The plot below compares the pace of the runners during different segments of the race, compared to their average pace over the entire race. For example, the violin plot labelled '0-10km' measures the (pace for first 10k, (sec/km)) minus (entire race pace (sec/km)). The big bump at about -5 means that a lot of people ran their first 10k at a pace that was about 5 seconds/km faster than their overall race pace. You can filter these results by age, gender, and finishing time. You can also change which segment you're comparing against by clicking on the title of the plot.
